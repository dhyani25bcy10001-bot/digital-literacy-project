## Project Overview

This Digital Literacy Project is designed to help students like us become more confident and responsible users of technology in our daily lives. As a Student Digital Ambassador, the aim of this project is not just to complete tasks, but to understand how digital tools can actually make our academic journey easier and more productive.

The project is divided into five tasks, each focusing on a different aspect of digital literacy. It starts with creating an infographic to spread awareness about what digital literacy means and why it matters. Then, it involves building a professional online presence using platforms like GitHub, LinkedIn, and Kaggle. It also includes exploring coding and collaboration tools such as HackerRank and Google Forms, which are useful for learning and teamwork.

Another important part of the project is learning how to communicate professionally through emails and understanding proper social media behavior. Finally, the project highlights cyber safety by studying a real-world scenario and learning how to stay protected online.

Overall, this project helped me understand how to use digital tools more effectively and responsibly, which will be useful throughout my college life and future career.

## Module 1 Summary

Presentation software plays an important role in digital literacy by helping users communicate ideas effectively using visuals, text, and multimedia. Tools like Canva, Prezi, Miro, GIF, and CoolText make it easier to create engaging and professional content without advanced technical skills.

Canva is widely used for designing presentations, posters, and infographics with ready-made templates. Prezi offers a unique zooming interface that makes presentations more interactive and dynamic. Miro is a collaborative platform that allows multiple users to work together in real-time, making it ideal for brainstorming and group projects. GIFs are animated images that help make content more engaging and easier to understand. CoolText is useful for creating attractive text-based logos and headings.

In the context of digital literacy, these tools help students improve creativity, communication, and collaboration skills. They also enable users to present information clearly and professionally, which is essential in academic and professional environments.

## Module 2 Summary
Building a digital portfolio is an important part of digital literacy, as it helps students create a professional online identity and showcase their skills. Platforms like LinkedIn, GitHub, and Kaggle are useful for networking, sharing projects, and developing technical skills.

Other platforms such as Quora, Stack Overflow, and ResearchGate help in learning, problem-solving, and academic growth. Additionally, TechGig, TED-Ed, and Wiki Education support skill development and knowledge sharing.

Overall, these platforms help students improve their skills, build credibility, and prepare for future opportunities.
